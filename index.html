eDNA Explorer

eDNA Explorer is a web-based platform to store and analyze environmental DNA (eDNA) samples. With eDNA Explorer we can help answer the questions, using eDNA, of what lived in a given area and what the environmental conditions were like at the time of sampling. Currently it takes in sequencing data to assign taxonomies via the Tronko pipeline. Environmental metadata is associated with samples' locations and dates using the TerraDactyl pipeline.

Prospering Backyards

From the site: This project is a community based initiative that is working to heal the lead-contaminated land surrounding the now-defunct Exide Industries in Vernon. This includes areas in East Los Angeles, Boyle Heights, Vernon, Commerce, Maywood, Huntington Park, and Bell, where thousands of families live affecting their health.

Safecast

Safecast was founded in the wake of the Fukushima meltdown in order to crowdsource tracking of nuclear fallout. I was heavily active in collecting radiation data, and analyzing geospatial patterns of fallout, back in 2011 - 2013.

DECO

This is a collaboration I started back in 2010 to use the camera chips in Android phones to run a distributed cosmic-ray detector array. It lives on at the University of Wisconsin at Madison.
